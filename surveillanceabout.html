About Internet Surveillance

With modern times and further steps into the age of the internet, the amount of surveillance performed on the internet has become a topic of major concern amongst many individuals. Companies such as Amazon, Facebook, and Google regularly collect data on its users, usually utilizing it in their comprehensive advertising algorithms. However, some utilizations of internet surveillance are far more sinister, using their collected data to track users and use what they collect against them in numerous ways.

Internet surveillance is typically defined as the monitoring of an individual's online activities and behavior, and is often performed by governments and corporations. There are many reasons why many people consider internet surveillance to be necessary. However, there are also multiple concerns that can be raised about internet surveillance.
